CS50's Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python

My projects and problem sets from Harvard's CS50 AI course. All projects are written in Python.

Librares used - Pygame, scikit-learn, TensorFlow, OpenCV, Natural Language Tool Kit, Hugging Face Transformers

Week 0 — Search

Projects: Degrees, Tic-Tac-Toe
Tools/Libraries: Pygame
Covered problem-solving via search algorithms like BFS, DFS, and Minimax, using them to find the shortest path between two people and to build an unbeatable Tic-Tac-Toe AI.

Week 1 — Knowledge

Projects: Knights, Minesweeper
Covered how AI represents and reasons about knowledge using propositional logic, inference rules, and model checking to solve logic puzzles and play Minesweeper safely.

Week 2 — Uncertainty

Projects: PageRank, Heredity
Covered probability theory, Bayesian networks, and Markov models, applying them to rank web pages by importance and to predict the likelihood of inherited genetic traits.

Week 3 — Optimization

Project: Crossword
Covered optimization techniques such as local search, linear programming, and constraint satisfaction, using backtracking search to automatically generate crossword puzzles.

Week 4 — Learning

Projects: Shopping, Nim
Covered supervised and reinforcement learning, including nearest-neighbor classification and Q-learning, to predict online shopping behavior and train an AI to play Nim.

Week 5 — Neural Networks

Project: Traffic
Covered the fundamentals of neural networks and convolutional neural networks, building an image classifier to identify traffic signs from street sign images.

Week 6 — Language

Projects: Parser, Attention
Covered natural language processing, including context-free grammars, n-grams, and transformer attention mechanisms, to parse sentence structure and analyze masked-language predictions.
